March 10 to March 16, 2026 Storm Viewer

An intense Kona low developed northwest of the islands between March 10 and 16, drawing heavy tropical moisture northward to produce days of severe rain and thunderstorms. This historic storm caused widespread destruction across the Hawaiian Islands, resulting in significant damage and major disruptions to power and utility networks. The Hawaii Climate Data Portal (HCDP) has now finalized updated daily rainfall maps to help the community, researchers, and recovery teams understand the full scale and intensity of the event.

Storm Highlight

Haleakalā Extreme Rainfall

During this historic storm, Maui was hit the hardest, with peak rainfall totals reaching 62 inches in localized areas. The upper elevations of Haleakalā experienced the most extreme rainfall, with 33.2 inches falling at the Haleakalā Summit Hawaiʻi Mesonet station during the 24-hour period beginning March 13 at 8:30 AM, nearly double the highest 24-hour rainfall previously recorded there. This amount greatly exceeded NOAA’s 24-hour 1000-year rainfall frequency estimate of 19.7 inches.

Rainfall was even higher at the Kuiki Hawaiʻi Mesonet station on the east rim of Haleakalā crater, where 36.42 inches fell in 24 hours beginning at 6:00 PM on March 13. This also greatly exceeded the NOAA 24-hour 1000-year estimate of 28.5 inches.
Reference: Perica, S., Martin, D., Lin, B., Parzybok, T., Riley, D., Yekta, M., Hiner, L., Chen, L.-C., Brewer, D., Yan, F., Maitaria, K., Trypaluk, C., & Bonnin, G. (2011). NOAA Atlas 14, Volume 4, Version 3: Precipitation-Frequency Atlas of the United States, Hawaiian Islands. Silver Spring, MD: National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, National Weather Service.
Storm Highlight

Puʻuwaʻawaʻa Extreme Wind

This storm generated extreme winds in some areas. The highest wind speeds were measured on Hawaiʻi Island on March 14. That day, the Kaiāulu Puʻuwaʻawaʻa Hawaiʻi Mesonet station recorded wind speeds that frequently exceeded 100 mph between midnight and 6:45 AM, averaged 105 mph between 4:10 AM and 4:25 AM, and experienced a peak wind gust of 135.4 mph at 4:20 AM.
