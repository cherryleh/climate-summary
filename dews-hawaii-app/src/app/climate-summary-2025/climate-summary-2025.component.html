Monthly Breakdown

New Records

August

August 2025 marked the driest August Hawaii has seen in the last 36 years.

Monthly Anomalies

11 out of 12 months drier than average

Other than January, all months in 2025 received below average rainfall. All 12 months saw higher than average temperature.

Dryest Areas

158 days

South Molokaʻi experienced 158 consecutive days with rainfall below 0.04 inches (1 mm), spanning from May 1 to October 5, 2025. North Kihei had the highest number of dry days in 2025 with 344 days without rain.

Data and disclaimer

Data in this report are derived from gridded climate products based on in situ measurements across Hawaiʻi and generated using various statistical and interpolation methods. Each product includes inherent uncertainty that is not explicitly quantified in the summary statistics presented here. Products and their associated metadata used in this report are available through the HCDP data access page. Additional information and citation guidance are available on the HCDP How to Cite page.
